# Novelty Boundary Map

## Crowded Territory

- Bigger multimodal encoders.
- Sensor dropout or masked-modality training alone.
- Generic Bayesian, ensemble, or conformal uncertainty gates.
- Failure detection without recovery-action consequences.
- Hardware-only multimodal sensing improvements.
- V4-style static composition rules without calibration, false-alarm suppression, fixed-risk accounting, or temporal desynchronization modeling.

## Claimed Boundary

Sensor failure compositionality models cross-sensor interaction terms that decide whether individually recoverable failures become a different, non-additive recovery problem when combined. The v5 claim is specifically risk-calibrated: a method must improve success, safety, damage, interaction F1, calibration, fixed-risk utility, and ablation necessity.

## What Would Falsify The Claim

If sensor dropout, independent fault detection, Bayesian fusion, robust single-worst-sensor policies, learned latent classifiers, mixture-of-experts routing, causal sensor graphs, risk-aware reconfiguration, or v4 composition rules match v5 hard success, safety, interaction F1, calibration, fixed-risk coverage, and utility under paired failures, the paper should be killed or reframed.

## Current Decision

The v5 local benchmark clears the frozen empirical gates, so the paper is `STRONG_REVISE`. It remains not submission-ready without external validation.
